Professional Summary of Research Interests and Expertise

I'm a highly motivated and passionate bioinformatician, data scientist and researcher naturally driven by curiosity to use mathematical, statistical and computational methods to answer fundamental and applied research questions in biodiversity science, evolutionary biology, ecology, genomics and bioinformatics, particularly related to molecular species identification and discovery through DNA barcoding and other DNA-based approaches.

Current Projects

Theoretical Aspects of DNA-based Taxon Identification

  • Developing methods for better visualization and inference of the DNA barcode gap

Building the Reference Library of Life

  • Constructing the largest DNA barcode reference sequence library for North American butterflies

Development of R Software Packages and Shiny Web Apps for Molecular Biodiversity Assessment

  • Improving the HACSim (Haplotype Accumulation Curve Simulator) nonparametric stochastic (Monte Carlo) local search optimization algorithm
